The Big Idea

EVERYTHING WORKS.

NOTHING STANDS APART.

For more than a century, Louis Sullivan's "form follows function" shaped how we build and design. But function was the frontier then. Today, advanced manufacturing, shared supply chains, and decades of design methodologies have raised the floor so high that looking good, and working well are no longer a strategy. It's the most basic expectation.

In a market filled with competent options, customers don't choose a product based on form and function alone. They choose because it has meaning— it's the accumulated weight of a thousand decisions that tell them whether a company stands for something worth remembering or not. Brand defines meaning, and design makes meaning visible. Your products are always communicating on behalf of your brand, what are they saying? If your brand has ever claimed to be one thing, but the products told another story, this book was written for you.

Algorithmic Polarization

The systems that decide who gets seen don't reward the reasonable middle. Standing for nothing is the fastest way to disappear. The question is what you're willing to stand for.

The Impact of AI

AI is rapidly commoditizing form and function. When every option looks capable and considered, form stops being the reason anyone picks you. So why does your customer choose your brand over another that is cheaper and looks equally competent?

About the Author

Jayson Simeon

FOUNDER, DESIGNER, AUTHOR

Jayson is a design and brand strategist with more than twenty years of experience spanning consulting and in-house leadership roles across fifteen industries. An award-winning designer, he has built his career at the intersection of brand strategy and product design, working with companies where the stakes of getting that relationship right are measured in market share, not applause. That practitioner credibility is the backbone of Form Follows Brand, a book rooted not in theory alone but in the hard-won clarity that comes from making real product decisions under real constraints.

Jayson is the founder of Adaptitude, where he helps companies build the strategic alignment between brand and design that most organizations talk about but few achieve. He lives in Austin, Texas, where he continues to refine the frameworks and philosophies that make brand-led design feel less like abstraction and more like common sense.
